Welcome!

My name is Alexa Fernando. I am a graduate student at the University of Hawaii at Mānoa where I study electrical engineering with a focus in systems and data science and a concentration in energy. My passion is to use electrical engineering to help solve some of the most critical problems in the world.

About Me.

Ever since I was a child, I understood the importance of being a good steward for the environment. My passion to study STEM and determination to help fight climate change led me to pursue a degree at the University of Hawaii in Electrical Engineering Systems with a concentration in energy. Through my studies in university and work experience at the STEM Pre-Academy and WSP, I continually refine my engineering skills such as circuit design, programming, and problem-solving. Putting these skills to use while I worked as a lab manager for the Renewable Energy Design lab in my third year of school was a rewarding experience. At the RED lab, I managed other engineering students as we worked on a smart hydroponics system that was implemented by STEM Pre-Academy to teach engineering and science to students at Washington Middle School. Currently, I am working on a Lithium-ion battery project with the Hawaii Natural Energy Institute (HNEI), where I am performing circuit design and programming to build a new type of li-ion segmented cell that improves upon current battery technology. Project and work experience in RED Lab, STEM Pre-Academy, WSP, and HNEI motivate me to use my degree to build a career where I can make a difference in the future of our planet and inspire other young women to pursue electrical engineering.
